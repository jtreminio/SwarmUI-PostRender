# Film Emulation / LPP Tetrachrome 400

Overall character: warm red-tinted shadows, soft highlight rolloff, and stronger greens and blues.

## Realistic Photographs

| Area | Excels at | Poor at | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Portraits and skin | warm daylight portraits, editorial beauty, and romantic lifestyle shots | cool-balanced studio skin, deeper complexions in dim light, and makeup or wardrobe color reference | Skin stays readable while the LUT steers complexion temperature, so it works best when the source already fits the intended mood. Deeper skin in dim scenes may print heavier. |
| Nature and foliage | dense botanical scenes, wet forests, and vivid spring landscapes | scenes that depend on exact green separation | The LUT gives greens extra energy without fully breaking them apart, so foliage-heavy frames feel more alive. |
| Sky, water, and cool light | cold skies, twilight blues, ocean scenes, and steel-toned night work | strict color-accurate cool scenes | Blue holds up well or intensifies, which helps night and winter imagery, but any hue rotation can push skies away from strict realism. |
| Warm materials and light | brick, terracotta, wood, sunset light, and warm practical interiors | scenes where warm brand colors must stay exact | Warm subjects stay attractive, though the LUT still nudges them toward its preferred palette. |
| Contrast and fidelity | general cinematic grading where you want a finished look without an extreme curve swing | strict reference or color-managed neutral workflows | The tonal move is moderate, but the LUT still bakes in a clear palette preference. |

## Illustrations

| Area | Excels at | Poor at | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Character art | painterly portrait art, cover illustrations, and expressive character work | palette-matched turnarounds and makeup-accurate or color-scripted faces | The LUT makes skin and warm accents feel more authored, which is excellent for mood but less ideal for exact palette preservation. |
| Backgrounds and environments | storybook streets, nostalgic interiors, and warm-world backgrounds | environments that need fully neutral atmospheric color | Environment work succeeds best when the scene already leans toward the LUTs preferred palette instead of fighting it. |
| Accent colors | accent colors built around greens, blues, and reds | strict brand palettes and locked swatch sets | Hue emphasis is not evenly distributed, so some accents get extra life while others are pushed into the background. |
| Line art and grayscale | line art, comic inks, and grayscale illustration with some room for stylization | assets that need absolutely untouched neutral ramps | The LUT does not disturb neutrals too aggressively, but it is still a look transform rather than a utility conversion. |
| Graphic and vector work | stylized poster and cover work | exact brand-color applications | Even in moderate cases, the LUT is still a creative grade rather than a neutral finishing step. |
