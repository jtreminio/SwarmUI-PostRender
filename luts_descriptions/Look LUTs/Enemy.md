# Look LUTs / Enemy

Overall character: controlled blacks, warm mids and highlights, soft highlight rolloff, stronger reds, and softer cyans and teals and blues.

## Realistic Photographs

| Area | Excels at | Poor at | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Portraits and skin | stylized portraiture, music-video fashion, and concept-led editorials | natural complexions, especially across mixed lighting, and deeper complexions in shadow | Skin and near-skin hues are being remapped rather than simply polished, so the look reads as intentional styling instead of neutral portrait finishing. |
| Nature and foliage | graphic landscapes, deliberately altered foliage, and concept-heavy location work | natural botanical color, travel realism, and foliage that must stay recognizably local | The LUT does not preserve natural foliage relationships, so outdoor scenes succeed only when that stylization is welcome. |
| Sky, water, and cool light | synthetic haze, twilight atmosphere, neon-mixed skies, and stylized night scenes | tropical cyan water, cyan LEDs, and clean daylight sky separation | Cyan is heavily compressed, so sky and water drift toward an invented atmosphere instead of vivid realism. |
| Warm materials and light | production design, brick, rust, sodium-lit interiors, and warm set dressing | product oranges, neutral beige references, and exact warm brand colors | Warm materials often take the LUT gracefully, but they still inherit a strong authorial bias rather than staying objective. |
| Contrast and fidelity | frames that want an aggressively finished look straight away | reference evaluation, neutral grading setup, and color-managed review | This LUT behaves like a creative look first, so it is better for committing to a style than for preserving optionality. |

## Illustrations

| Area | Excels at | Poor at | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Character art | painterly character posters, concept-heavy character art, and key visual work | turnaround sheets, palette-matched character bibles, and color-scripted faces | Character art benefits when the LUT is allowed to impose its own mood, but that same push works against exact palette control. |
| Backgrounds and environments | concept backgrounds, posterized cityscapes, altered environments, and title-card scenery | naturalistic environment paintovers, lush fantasy forests, and accurate daylight exteriors | Backgrounds can look striking when they lean into the transform, but they stop reading as physically neutral spaces. |
| Accent colors | accents built around reds | cyans and teals, blues, and magentas and violets when they need to stay bright and exact | Hue emphasis is highly uneven, so some accent families get a gift while others are deliberately pushed out. |
| Line art and grayscale | vintage print effects, distressed comics, and soft posterized monochrome art | neutral manga tones, UI diagrams, and clean black-ink line work | Neutrals and blacks are not left alone, so grayscale material becomes stylized instead of clinical. |
| Graphic and vector work | poster art, splash pages, title cards, and one-off marketing visuals | logos, icons, dashboards, and reusable vector systems | This LUT wants to create a finished look in one move, which helps hero art but fights flat design systems. |
