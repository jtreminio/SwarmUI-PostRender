# Look LUTs / Seven

Overall character: deep clean blacks, gentle highlight rolloff, stronger reds and blues, and softer cyans and teals and yellows and ambers.

## Realistic Photographs

| Area | Excels at | Poor at | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Portraits and skin | warm daylight portraits, editorial beauty, and romantic lifestyle shots | cool-balanced studio skin, deeper complexions in dim light, and makeup or wardrobe color reference | Skin-like oranges gain warmth and presence, which flatters many portraits but can overstate already-rosy faces. Deeper skin in dim scenes may print heavier. |
| Nature and foliage | restrained parks, late-summer fields, and naturalistic outdoor scenes | lush tropical foliage and bright emerald landscapes | Greens are pushed cooler, which can feel cinematic but starts to look synthetic on ordinary foliage. |
| Sky, water, and cool light | hazy coasts, subdued overcast, and calmer travel imagery | tropical cyan water and teal practical lighting | Blue holds up well or intensifies, which helps night and winter imagery, but any hue rotation can push skies away from strict realism. |
| Warm materials and light | brick, terracotta, wood, sunset light, and warm practical interiors | product oranges, calibration targets, and synthetic yellows | Warm materials get extra density and separation, which reads as cinematic but not fully neutral. |
| Contrast and fidelity | general cinematic grading where you want a finished look without an extreme curve swing | strict reference or color-managed neutral workflows | The tonal move is moderate, but the LUT still bakes in a clear palette preference. |

## Illustrations

| Area | Excels at | Poor at | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Character art | painterly portrait art, cover illustrations, and expressive character work | palette-matched turnarounds and makeup-accurate or color-scripted faces | The LUT makes skin and warm accents feel more authored, which is excellent for mood but less ideal for exact palette preservation. |
| Backgrounds and environments | storybook streets, nostalgic interiors, and warm-world backgrounds | cyberpunk teal scenes | Environment work succeeds best when the scene already leans toward the LUTs preferred palette instead of fighting it. |
| Accent colors | accent colors built around reds and blues | cyans and teals, yellows and ambers, and greens when they need to stay bright and exact | Hue emphasis is not evenly distributed, so some accents get extra life while others are pushed into the background. |
| Line art and grayscale | vintage print effects, soft posterization, and textured monochrome art | neutral manga tones, UI mockups, and crisp black-ink line work | Neutrals pick up a cast and the black point lifts, which softens grayscale material instead of keeping it clinical. |
| Graphic and vector work | retro poster treatments and mood boards | logo work, infographics, and palette-critical vector art | The transform is opinionated enough that clean graphic work stops looking intentionally flat. |
