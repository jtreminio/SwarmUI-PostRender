# Look LUTs / Rhythm

Overall character: controlled blacks, soft highlight rolloff, and stronger yellows and ambers and cyans and teals.

## Realistic Photographs

| Area | Excels at | Poor at | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Portraits and skin | warm daylight portraits, editorial beauty, and romantic lifestyle shots | cool-balanced studio skin and makeup or wardrobe color reference | Skin-like oranges gain warmth and presence, which flatters many portraits but can overstate already-rosy faces. |
| Nature and foliage | dense botanical scenes, wet forests, and vivid spring landscapes | scenes that depend on exact green separation | The LUT gives greens extra energy without fully breaking them apart, so foliage-heavy frames feel more alive. |
| Sky, water, and cool light | general sky work, cool fill light, and moderate coastal scenes | strict color-accurate cool scenes | Cool hues stay usable, but the LUT still imposes a visible bias on sky-water separation. |
| Warm materials and light | brick, terracotta, wood, sunset light, and warm practical interiors | scenes where warm brand colors must stay exact | Warm subjects stay attractive, though the LUT still nudges them toward its preferred palette. |
| Contrast and fidelity | general cinematic grading where you want a finished look without an extreme curve swing | strict reference or color-managed neutral workflows | The tonal move is moderate, but the LUT still bakes in a clear palette preference. |

## Illustrations

| Area | Excels at | Poor at | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Character art | painterly portrait art, cover illustrations, and expressive character work | palette-matched turnarounds | The LUT makes skin and warm accents feel more authored, which is excellent for mood but less ideal for exact palette preservation. |
| Backgrounds and environments | storybook streets, nostalgic interiors, and warm-world backgrounds | environments that need fully neutral atmospheric color | Environment work succeeds best when the scene already leans toward the LUTs preferred palette instead of fighting it. |
| Accent colors | accent colors built around yellows and ambers, cyans and teals, and greens | strict brand palettes and locked swatch sets | Hue emphasis is not evenly distributed, so some accents get extra life while others are pushed into the background. |
| Line art and grayscale | vintage print effects, soft posterization, and textured monochrome art | neutral manga tones, UI mockups, and crisp black-ink line work | Neutrals pick up a cast and the black point lifts, which softens grayscale material instead of keeping it clinical. |
| Graphic and vector work | retro poster treatments and mood boards | logo work, infographics, and palette-critical vector art | The transform is opinionated enough that clean graphic work stops looking intentionally flat. |
